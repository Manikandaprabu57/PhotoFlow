Existing Problems

• Manual photo identification and sorting leads to incorrect photo-to-guest matching and delivery errors

• Photographers struggle to efficiently organize and deliver large volumes of event photos to multiple guests

• Lack of intelligent automated photo matching and guest identification from bulk event photos

• Time mismatch between photo processing and guest delivery, causing delays and dissatisfaction

• High risk of data breach and unauthorized access to sensitive guest photos in unsecured photo sharing

• Manual email communication and photo delivery processes consume significant photographer time and resources

• No automated verification of photo quality and face recognition accuracy before guest delivery

• Photographers unable to provide real-time tracking and analytics on photo delivery status to guests

• Limited scalability due to manual processes, preventing photographers from handling multiple simultaneous events

• Lack of personalized guest experience and self-service photo retrieval options in traditional delivery methods

• Storage limitations and high local infrastructure costs for managing large event photo databases

• Inability to match faces across multiple event photos due to absence of AI-powered face recognition systems

• Poor guest engagement metrics and no insights into which photos are most popular among attendees

• Insecure payment and transaction handling in photo delivery and subscription-based services

• High operational overhead and low profit margins due to inefficient manual photo management workflows
